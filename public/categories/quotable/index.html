Ghost Rider

Sun, Aug 26, 2012
Over the last year or so I’ve been slowing working my way through Neil Peart’s Ghost Rider, his memoir of a motorcycle journey he took after losing his daughter and wife in a single year. Documenting his 55,000-mile ride through Canada, the United States, Mexico and Belize, Neil does a good job of not only communicating the alien feelings experienced within the depths of loss, but the frustration of having to live in this futile, superficial and mostly pathetic world.
