XOXO 2013 (Part 3)

Tue Oct 29, 2013

The final installment of XOXO posts, because a lot has gone on since then I also want to blog about

An aerial drone view of XOXO

{% youtube uf9rrskAV-A %}

Speaking drones, Chris Anderson, former editor of Wired, now runs a drone company, delivered the first talk of the festival. Afterwards I was already mentally buzzed and ready to get back to Dallas and do awesome things…

{% youtube ZeLF8LTePZI %}

The first speaker I was really looking forward to was Maciej Cegłowski. I’ve enjoyed his writing for a long time, and for almost as long a time, his bookmarking service. Turns out he is a great speaker as well, and used the writings of Thoreau as a launch point for an inspiring talk about character and authentic living in the modern age.

{% youtube eky5uKILXtM %}

The first surprise for me was Jack Cheng, who I had not heard of before. But his honesty about his motivations, process and financial realities immediately endeared me to him.

You don’t have to quit your job and go all in on your art. It’s something, that if it’s really about self-discovery, it’s something that is healing and something that is constantly at work and something you can never do enough of…

{% youtube F2gq_0zdmjU %}

I mentioned Jack Conte in my last post. Here is his talk. Don’t forget to check out his project for funding artists, patreon.

{% youtube K9NjntTUJ1Q %}

Jonathon Coulton, another musician…with similar themes…

I didn’t want to be ‘sad dad’ with unfulfilled dreams…Happiness is the only metric you need for judging your success. It’s your boss.

{% youtube xtcjRyoOGIs %}
